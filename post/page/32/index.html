Plusquamperfekt: How to use the past perfect in German

August 08, 2024
blog
If two events happened in the past, how would you know that one of them occurred earlier than the other? The answer — in German, at least — lies in a verb tense known as the Plusquamperfekt (past perfect).  The Plusquamperfekt allows you to talk about past events in chronological order. This makes it useful for telling stories that take place across a  timeline (as many stories do). For example, if you want to tell your German-speaking friends that you first attended aGerman course and then went to the cinema, the Plusquamperfekt helps to clarify the order of these events.
